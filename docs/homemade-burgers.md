# Homemade Burgers

> **Takeaway:** Simple ingredients, properly prepared toppings, and well-seasoned beef create a juicy diner-style burger with plenty of flavor. The Hidden Valley Ranch seasoning mixed directly into the beef gives these burgers their signature taste.

## Overview

A great burger isn't complicated, but consistency matters.

This recipe focuses on:

- Properly seasoned beef
- Thin, evenly shaped patties
- Fresh toppings prepared in advance
- Lightly toasted buns
- Quick cooking to keep the burgers juicy

The result is a classic backyard-style burger that pairs perfectly with fries, potato salad, or chips.

---

## Yield

**8 burgers**

> **Note:** Most hamburger buns come in packs of 8, which makes this recipe easy to portion.

---

## Ingredients

### For the Burgers

- 2 lbs lean ground beef
- 1 packet Hidden Valley Ranch dressing mix

### For the Buns

- 1 package hamburger buns
  - Sara Lee Hawaiian Style buns recommended

### Optional Cheese

- Kraft American Singles
- Or your favorite sliced cheese

### Toppings

- 1 red onion
- 1 head iceberg lettuce
- 1–2 ripe tomatoes
- Hamburger dill pickles
- Sliced jalapeños
- Yellow mustard
- Mayonnaise

---

## Prepare the Toppings First

Having everything ready before the meat cooks makes burger assembly much easier.

### Onions

Slice the red onion thinly.

A mandoline slicer works especially well for producing uniform slices.

Place in a bowl and refrigerate until serving.

### Lettuce

Prepare about half a head of iceberg lettuce.

You can:

- Shred it finely for classic burger topping texture.
- Leave some larger leaves for those who prefer full lettuce pieces.

Refrigerate until needed.

### Tomatoes

Slice tomatoes thinly.

Place in a bowl and refrigerate.

### Pickles & Jalapeños

Line a bowl with paper towels.

Place a healthy serving of pickles in one bowl and jalapeños in another.

Allow excess liquid to drain while you prepare everything else.

This helps keep burgers from becoming soggy.

---

## Prepare the Beef

Place the ground beef into a large mixing bowl.

Add:

- 1 packet Hidden Valley Ranch dressing mix

Mix thoroughly until the seasoning is evenly distributed throughout the meat.

### Divide the Meat

Divide the mixture:

1. In half
2. Then each half in half
3. Then each section in half again

You should end up with:

```text
8 equal portions
```

Roll each portion into a ball.

---

## Form the Patties

> **Tip:** Place small squares of wax paper between finished patties to prevent sticking.

Place a beef ball on a dinner plate.

Use a second plate to press the meat flat.

Aim for:

- Less than 1/2 inch thick
- Approximately 6 inches across

The patties should be larger than the bun.

This is intentional because burgers shrink while cooking.

Stack finished patties with wax paper between them.

---

## Heat the Pans

### Burger Pan

Start heating a large skillet before the patties are ready.

On an electric stove:

```text
Heat Level: 7
```

is a good starting point.

### Bun Pan (Optional)

A second skillet can be used to lightly toast the buns.

Not everyone likes toasted buns, but it adds flavor and helps prevent sogginess.

---

## Cook the Burgers

Place the patties into the hot skillet.

### Don't Flip Too Early

Wait until:

- Red juices begin pooling on the top surface of the burger.

This usually takes a few minutes.

Then flip.

### Control Excess Grease

If excessive grease accumulates:

- Wad up a paper towel.
- Hold with tongs.
- Carefully soak up excess fat.

### Cooking Time

When the heat is properly set:

```text
About 3 minutes per side
```

is usually enough.

Avoid overcooking.

A burger should remain juicy.

---

## Toast the Buns

If using toasted buns:

- Place buns in a hot skillet.
- Rotate occasionally.
- Watch carefully to prevent burning.

Light golden-brown is ideal.

---

## Add Cheese

Once burgers are finished cooking:

Transfer them to a plate to rest.

Place a slice of cheese on top immediately.

The residual heat will melt it perfectly.

---

## Build the Burger

Layer as desired:

1. Bottom bun
2. Burger patty
3. Cheese
4. Lettuce
5. Tomato
6. Onion
7. Pickles
8. Jalapeños
9. Mustard
10. Mayo
11. Top bun

---

## Optional Upgrades

### Texas Heat

Add:

- Extra jalapeños
- Pepper jack cheese
- Hot sauce

### Backyard Double Burger

Use two patties per burger.

Add an extra cheese slice between them.

### Steakhouse Version

Top with:

- Grilled onions
- Mushrooms
- Sharp cheddar

### Bacon Ranch

Add:

- Crispy bacon
- Extra ranch seasoning in the beef

---

## Serving Suggestions

Serve with:

- Homemade potato salad
- French fries
- Onion rings
- Baked beans
- Coleslaw
- Chips

---

## Greg's Burger Tips

- Prepare toppings before cooking.
- Make patties larger than the buns.
- Don't flip too early.
- Don't overcook.
- Let the burger rest briefly before serving.
- Toasting the buns is optional but highly recommended.

> **Greg's Tip:** Burgers sound simple, but making them quickly, cleanly, and consistently takes practice. Preparation is what separates a good burger from a great burger.
