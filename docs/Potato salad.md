# Classic Homestyle Potato Salad

> **Takeaway:** Tender potatoes, hard-boiled eggs, sweet relish, mustard, and sour cream come together for a creamy Southern-style potato salad that's perfect for cookouts, holidays, and family dinners.

## Overview

This potato salad balances creamy, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors while keeping enough texture from the potatoes and eggs to avoid becoming mashed.

The key is gently mixing the ingredients so some potato chunks remain intact, giving the salad a hearty homemade texture.

## Ingredients

- 5 medium potatoes (about the size of a closed fist)
- 6 hard-boiled eggs
- 1 yellow onion, diced medium-fine
- 2 heaping tablespoons sweet relish
- 3 tablespoons yellow mustard
- 3 tablespoons sour cream
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Potatoes

Peel the potatoes and cut them into approximately **3/4-inch pieces**.

Place in a large pot and boil until fork-tender. The potatoes should be soft enough to pierce easily but not falling apart.

Drain the potatoes in a colander and briefly rinse to stop the cooking process.

Transfer to a large mixing bowl.

### 2. Add the Vegetables

Add the diced yellow onion to the potatoes.

### 3. Build the Dressing

Add:

- Sweet relish
- Yellow mustard
- Sour cream

Season lightly with salt and black pepper.

### 4. Prepare the Eggs

Peel all six hard-boiled eggs.

Chop into bite-sized pieces and add them to the bowl.

### 5. Mix Carefully

Using a rubber spatula or large spoon, gently fold everything together.

**Do not overmix.**

The goal is to coat the potatoes evenly while leaving plenty of chunks intact.

A few broken pieces are fine and help create a naturally creamy texture.

### 6. Chill

Transfer the potato salad to a serving bowl or storage container.

Cover tightly and refrigerate until well chilled.

For best flavor, allow the potato salad to rest for at least a few hours before serving.

## Yield

- Approximately **2.5 quarts**
- Serves **8–10 people** as a side dish

## Optional Upgrades

### Extra Creamy Version

Add:

- 2–3 tablespoons mayonnaise
- An additional tablespoon of sour cream

### Deviled Egg Style

Sprinkle with:

- Paprika
- A little extra mustard
- Finely chopped egg whites on top

### Texas Kick

Add:

- Diced jalapeño
- A splash of pickle juice
- A dash of Cajun seasoning

### Garden Fresh

Mix in:

- Diced celery
- Chopped green onions
- Fresh parsley

## Serving Notes

This potato salad pairs especially well with:

- Barbecue
- Smoked brisket
- Fried fish
- Hamburgers
- Hot dogs
- Grilled chicken

The flavor improves after chilling, making it an excellent make-ahead side dish for gatherings and cookouts.

> **Greg's Tip:** The secret to great potato salad is texture. Mix gently enough that some potatoes remain in hearty chunks while others break down just enough to create a naturally creamy coating.
