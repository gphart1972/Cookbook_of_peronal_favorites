# Steakhouse-Style Baked Fish

> **Takeaway:** Frozen fish + steakhouse vegetables + slow baking = a savory, one-pan dinner with almost no cleanup.

## Ingredients

- 4 frozen fish fillets (cod, pollock, haddock, or tilapia)
- 1–2 tbsp oil (for coating the pan)
- 1/2 cup red onion, rough-cut
- 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
- 1/2 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
- 1 bunch asparagus
- Seasoning of choice:
  - Salt
  - Pepper
  - Garlic powder
  - Cajun seasoning
  - Lemon pepper
  - Or your favorite blend

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to **325°F (163°C)**.
2. Coat the bottom of a **9×13 baking dish** (glass or CorningWare) with oil.
3. Place the frozen fish fillets in the pan.
4. Scatter the onions, mushrooms, Brussels sprouts, and asparagus around and on top of the fish.
5. Season generously.
6. Cover tightly with aluminum foil.
7. Bake for **1 hour**.
8. Serve and enjoy.

## Optional Upgrades

### Compound Butter
Place a pat of compound butter on each fillet before baking for a richer flavor.

### Steakhouse Seasoning
Use a steakhouse-style seasoning blend to give the fish and vegetables a grilled steakhouse flavor profile.

### Broth or White Wine
Add a small splash of broth or white wine before covering the pan to help keep everything moist.

### Browned Vegetables
Remove the foil during the last **10 minutes** of cooking to lightly brown the vegetables.

### Make It a Full Meal
Add small, diced baby potatoes to the pan before baking. Cut them into small pieces so they'll cook through in the same amount of time.

## Notes

- No thawing required.
- Works well with most mild white fish.
- Easy to customize with seasonal vegetables.
- Great for meal prep and leftovers.
