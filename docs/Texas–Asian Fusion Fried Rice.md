# Texas–Asian Fusion Fried Rice

> **Takeaway:** A wok-style fried rice that blends Texas heat, Japanese umami, and bright citrus flavors into one deeply savory dish. Crisp, spicy, slightly tangy, and packed with vegetables.

## Overview

This fried rice balances **umami, heat, citrus, and caramelization**.

The Japanese soy-based BBQ sauce provides depth and sweetness, Tajín adds brightness, Crystal hot sauce brings Louisiana-style heat, and fresh orange juice ties everything together with a clean, aromatic finish.

## Ingredients

- 2–3 cups cooked rice (day-old preferred)
- 2 cups small broccoli florets
- 1/2 cup sliced carrots
- 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup peas
- 1/2 cup diced tomatoes, well-drained
- 2–3 eggs (optional, but recommended)
- 1–2 tbsp Japanese soy-based BBQ sauce
- 1 tsp Tajín seasoning
- 1–2 tbsp fresh orange juice
- Crystal hot sauce, to finish
- 2–3 tbsp butter
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Green onions, optional garnish

## Signature Cooking Method

### 1. Build the Flavor Base

Heat butter in a wok or large skillet over **medium heat**.

Once melted, add:

- Carrots
- Broccoli
- Jalapeños
- Peas
- Tomatoes

Cover with a lid and allow the vegetables to steam until slightly tender.

### 2. Add the Rice

Add the cooked rice and break up any clumps.

Spread the rice out across the pan to maximize contact with the cooking surface.

### 3. Add the Umami Layer

Pour in the Japanese soy-based BBQ sauce.

Allow the sauce to caramelize into the rice.

**Do not stir constantly.**

The goal is to develop crispy, toasted bits of rice for texture and flavor.

### 4. Add the Eggs

Crack in the eggs and stir until fully incorporated and cooked through.

### 5. Add Fresh Orange Juice

Splash in **1–2 tablespoons** of fresh orange juice.

Allow it to sizzle for **10–15 seconds** before stirring.

This brightens the entire dish and adds a subtle citrus finish.

### 6. Final Seasoning

Remove from heat and finish with:

- Tajín seasoning
- Crystal hot sauce
- Green onions (optional)

Taste and adjust:

- More BBQ sauce for additional umami
- More Tajín for brightness
- More Crystal for heat

## Flavor Profile

This dish combines:

- **Umami** from the Japanese BBQ sauce
- **Heat** from jalapeños and Crystal hot sauce
- **Brightness** from Tajín and fresh orange juice
- **Sweetness and caramelization** from the vegetables and sauce
- **Texture** from crispy rice and tender-crisp vegetables

## Serving Notes

Serve immediately while the rice is still crisp.

For the best results:

- Use day-old rice.
- Allow the rice to sit against the pan long enough to develop crispy edges.
- Taste and adjust seasoning before serving.

The finished dish should land in a sweet spot between:

> **Savory • Bright • Spicy • Crispy**
